Definitions

σ (sigma)
Sample standard deviation of daily yield changes, computed over the full available history (ddof=1).
Excess kurtosis
The 4th standardized moment minus 3. A true normal distribution has excess kurtosis = 0. Positive values indicate fatter tails than the normal — more extreme events than the bell curve predicts.
Value-at-Risk (VaR)
Under the normal assumption, 1-day VaR at confidence level CL is μ − z·σ, where z is the standard normal quantile for that level (z = 1.645 at 95%, 2.326 at 99%, 3.090 at 99.9%). This is the most common business use of the normal distribution in treasury and risk management.
Q-Q plot
Plots observed data quantiles against expected normal quantiles. If the data is normal, points fall on the 45° line. Points pulling away in the tails signal fat tails.
Sigma band analysis
For each integer k (1–8), the normal model predicts P(|Z| > k) × n observations. Actual counts far exceeding this prediction is the "impossible becomes possible" scenario.

Methodology Caveats

The normal distribution assumption understates tail risk in financial returns — this is a documented empirical finding, not a modelling choice. Real daily yield changes exhibit excess kurtosis (4–8 in historical Treasury data), which means a VaR sized on the normal model will be breached more often than the model predicts. This dashboard surfaces that gap explicitly.
