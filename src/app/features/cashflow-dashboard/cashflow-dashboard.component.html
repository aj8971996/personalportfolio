This dashboard is organized around 4 questions a treasury or finance team would ask in a weekly liquidity review. Each tab answers one question with only the data that serves it. No competing context.

Tab 1 combines rate KPIs and the yield curve intentionally. Fed funds rate, 10Y yield, and the 2Y–10Y spread all answer the same question: what is the cost of liquidity and is the market signaling stress? Together they complete the picture; separately they're just numbers.

Tabs 2–4 are deliberately separated. Cash position (forecast + anomalies), waterfall decomposition, and variance analysis each answer a distinct management question. Grouping them would obscure the answer to each.

Forecast methodology: Exponential smoothing (α = 0.25), 8-week horizon, 80% confidence bands from residual standard error. Anomaly detection uses 2σ z-score on historical actuals.

Variance thresholds: On-track ≤ ±5% · Watch ±5–15% · Breach > ±15%.

In production, this connects to Workday, Coupa, or your TMS with automated weekly refresh and threshold-breach alerting. The FRED data is a public proxy for internal balances: the analytical pattern is identical; only the data source changes.
